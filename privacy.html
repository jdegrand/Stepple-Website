Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 27, 2026
App: Stepple
Developer: Joey DeGrand

This policy explains what information Stepple collects, how it is used, and your rights regarding that information.

1. Where Your Data Lives

Stepple stores all app data locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. This includes:

  • Your virtual pet's name, species, and activity history
  • Daily fitness summaries derived from Apple Health (step counts, active calories, sleep hours, workout records, flights climbed, walking distance)
  • App preferences (daily goal, background, customization settings)
  • Accolade and achievement progress
  • Lifetime activity totals and retirement history for each pet

By default, your health data, pet data, and app preferences are not transmitted to any server operated by the developer, and all automatic processing of your activity and pet state happens entirely on your device.

Stepple may offer optional features that, if you turn them on, transmit data you choose to Apple's iCloud services. These features are opt-in and the exact data they share is disclosed before you enable them. They include:

  • Backup ownership verification (Section 4) — a single anonymous token.
  • The referral program (Section 3) — only if you visit Settings → Refer a Friend.
  • Optional social features such as leaderboards, shared goals, or activity comparisons. If you enable one of these features, it may share information the app stores about your activity and your virtual pet — including (without limitation) activity values read from Apple Health, derived progress metrics, virtual pet state, achievement and accolade data, and connection identifiers — through Apple's CloudKit service with members of leaderboards you explicitly join. The exact data shared by each feature will be clearly described before you enable it. Any such feature will be off by default and can be disabled at any time in Settings.

No data described in this policy is ever sold, used for advertising, or linked to your name, email, or Apple ID.

2. Apple Health Data

Stepple requests read-only access to the following Apple Health data types:

  • Active Energy Burned
  • Step Count
  • Sleep Analysis
  • Workouts
  • Flights Climbed
  • Walking + Running Distance

Stepple does not write any data to Apple Health. Health data is used primarily to drive in-app pet mechanics and is never sold, shared with advertising networks, or used for advertising purposes. Health data transmission is limited to optional features you explicitly enable (see Section 1); no automatic transmission occurs.

You can revoke Health access at any time in Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → Stepple.

3. iCloud Services

Stepple uses three Apple iCloud services:

iCloud Key-Value Storage — stores a single anonymous token used for backup ownership verification. This token contains no personal information.

CloudKit (optional features) — Stepple uses Apple's CloudKit database for optional features you choose to enable.

Referral program: if you visit Settings → Refer a Friend, Stepple creates a record containing only your anonymous referral code, your anonymous iCloud user ID, and a count of successful redemptions. If you never visit that page, no CloudKit record is created by the referral program.

Optional social features (such as leaderboards or shared goals), if enabled, may store information the app has about your activity and virtual pet — including (without limitation) activity values, progress metrics, pet state, achievement and accolade data, and connection identifiers — in CloudKit records shared through Apple's CloudKit service with members of leaderboards you have explicitly joined. The specific data stored will be disclosed at the point of enabling each feature. Data stored in CloudKit is subject to Apple's CloudKit security model and privacy practices.

To delete your CloudKit data, email support@stepple.fit and the developer will remove it manually.

Apple StoreKit — if you subscribe to Stepple+, Apple manages your subscription and payment information directly. The developer does not receive or store your payment details.

None of these services is used for analytics or advertising. All are operated by Apple under their privacy policies.

4. Backup Files

Stepple automatically saves signed backup files to your device's local storage after each daily sync, retaining only the most recent few. These files never leave your device. Backups are cryptographically signed with a device-specific key derived from an anonymous token stored in your iCloud Key-Value Storage, which means a backup file could only be restored on devices that share the same iCloud account. The developer has no access to these files.

5. Subscription Data

Stepple+ subscriptions are processed entirely by Apple via In-App Purchase. The developer may receive anonymized transaction receipts and anonymized subscription lifecycle notifications from Apple (for example: purchase, renewal, cancellation, refund) to verify entitlement and measure subscription performance. These contain Apple's anonymous transaction and product identifiers only. No name, email, or payment information is shared with the developer.

6. Diagnostic Logs

Stepple writes diagnostic logs to your device to help troubleshoot bugs. These logs describe app behavior but do not contain your actual step counts, calorie totals, sleep hours, workout details, or other health values. Your pet's name is also excluded from logs.

Logs are kept for 7 days and then automatically deleted. Logs never leave your device automatically. The only way logs are shared is if you tap Settings → Report a Bug and explicitly choose to include them — they are then encrypted and attached to an email you send to the developer. Attaching logs is optional; you can send a bug report without them. The encrypted file can only be read by the developer.

7. Analytics and Advertising

Stepple may send anonymous information about how the app is used — including, for example, which screens are viewed, which features are used, onboarding progress, paywall interactions, subscription lifecycle events, retention and engagement milestones, and error and performance diagnostics. This information helps the developer understand usage patterns, measure subscription conversions, diagnose problems, and improve the app.

When such data is sent, it is tied only to a random identifier generated on your device and is not linked to your name, email, Apple ID, or health data. No health or fitness values are ever included. No pet names are ever included.

This data may be processed directly by the developer or by third-party analytics, diagnostics, and paywall service providers acting as data processors under the developer's instructions. The current list of processors is maintained at stepple.fit/processors.

Stepple does not use any advertising network, and no data described in this policy is used for advertising.

Health and fitness data is never included in the anonymous analytics described in this section. Diagnostic logs stored on your device (described in Section 6) record only broad ranges (e.g. "1-5k steps," "300-500 cal") rather than exact values — even if you share logs with the developer for bug reports, your actual activity levels are never disclosed. Any other transmission of health and fitness data is limited to optional social features you explicitly enable (see Section 1).

8. Children

Stepple is not directed at children under 13. As described throughout this policy, the app does not knowingly collect personal information from any user, including children.

9. Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes materially, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new effective date. Continued use of the app after such changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

10. Contact

Questions about this policy can be directed to: support@stepple.fit
